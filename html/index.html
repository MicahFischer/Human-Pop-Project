Human Population Timeline

1793-2009

1810

French Chef Nicolas Appert invents airtight food preservation (canning) allowing people to eat fruits and vegetables year round.

1825

The first steam-powered railway travels across Great Britain revolutionizing transportation.

1843

Edwin Chadwick starts the Public Health Movement in Britain to improve the sanitation and combat deadly water borne diseases like Cholera, Typhoid, and Typhus.

1848

World’s first Women’s rights convention held in Seneca Falls, NY.

1858

Louis Pasteur links germs to fermentation and disease leading to the pasteurization of food and the use of sterilization and antiseptics and a new understanding of microbiology.

1869

The first Transcontinental Railroad is completed allowing the quick transport of food and other goods across the U.S.

1879

Thomas Edison demonstrates his first incandescent light bulb, leading to the opening of the first utility plant a few years later.

1893

New Zealand is the first country to grant women equal voting rights- 27 years before the U.S.

1903

The Wright brothers first flight.

1908

German Chemist, Fritz Haber, discovers how to use nitrogen as fertilizer enabling a revolution in agriculture that begins to feed millions more.

1923

American Clarence Birdseye invented flash freezing technique to preserve food.

1929

The Great Depression results in a “baby bust” birth rates begin to decline.

1939

DDT is introduced marking the start of heavy pesticide use on crops in the U.S. It was later banned in 1972.

1940’s

Antibiotics are developed transforming medicine worldwide and increasing life expectancy.

1946

Start of the post-war “Baby Boom” in the U.S and Europe. Lasts until 1964 with 3.8 children per family average.

1960

Sri Lanka announces the first female Prime Minister.

1964

Vaccine for measles is available; child deaths are reduced worldwide.

1970

First Earth Day

1980

Smallpox is eradicated (it has reappeared in recent years but was non-existent for several decades).

1985

The world's ecological footprint begins to exceed the Earth’s biocapacity.

1992

Cod begin to disappear off the coast of Canada due to overfishing.

1995

Most deforestation of the Amazon in one year is recorded. 11,216 sq. miles (bigger than the state of Massachusetts).

1997

The first hybrid car (gas and electric) goes on sale. The Toyota Prius is mass produced.

2000

The United Nations adopts the Millennium Development Goals to improve the standard of living for all by 2015

2009

World Hunger reaches a new high with an estimated 1 billion people that do not have enough to eat.

1798

English Economist Thomas Malthus published “An Essay on The Principles of Population” warning the dangers of human overpopulation.

1820’s

Lowell, Massachusetts. America’s first planned factory town is built beginning the Industrial Revolution in America.

1834

Cyrus McCormick creates the first mechanical reaper that allows farmers to triple their output, thus allowing for more crops to feed the growing population.

1845

The Irish Potato Famine begins, killing more than 1 million people and forcing a mass migration due to starvation.

1855

Charles Goodyear (Goodyear tires and other products) produces the first rubber condom.

1865

The U.S. Supreme Court strikes down the ban of contraceptives.

1873

The U.S Congress passes the Comstock laws making all forms of contraception illegal.

1885

The first combustible engine is developed by German engineer Gottlieb Daimler.

1902

American Engineer Willis Carrier creates the first air conditioner, which leads to population migration to the southern states.

1908

Ford’s first Model T rolls off the assembly line. 15 Million more were sold in the next 20 years.

1917

GE launches the first refrigerator keeping food cold and reducing foodborne illness.

1928

The Spanish Flu epidemic kills over 50 million people worldwide.

1937

The first washing machine is introduced. This invention has greatly reduced the amount of time spent washing clothes.

1939-1945

WWII results in 60 million deaths.

1945

The United Nations is formed to promote “respect human rights and freedoms for all”.

1955

FIrst vaccine for Polio is created beginning mass inoculations

1962

Rachel Carson published “Silent Spring” drawing attention to the dangers of pesticides on human health and the environment

1968

Book “The Population Bomb” by Paul Ehrlich is published warning of the dangers of exponential Population growth.

1978

The first “Test Tube Baby” is born. More than 4 million babies have been born since using in-vitro fertilization.

1980

China’s population reaches 1 billion and the government establishes the “one child” policy to limit population growth.

1989

The World Wide Web is created by British Physicist Timothy Berners-Lee allowing for rapid, global communications

1994

World Summit in Cairo, Egypt establishes that the key to improving poverty and stabilizing world populations is women empowerment.

1997

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is discovered. A floating junkyard of mostly plastic that stretches for hundreds of miles.

1998

Coral bleaching for high sea temperatures destroys 16% of the worlds coral reef in one year. 50% of the reefs in the Indian Ocean are already destroyed.

2008

The United Nations announces that more than 50% of the World's population live in cities.
